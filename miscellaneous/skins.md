# Skins

Assetto Corsa has a very clever system to change textures on a car. If you want to change the texture like the livery based on the skin choice you can create a folder inside the skins folder which must contain a livery.png and ui\_skin.json in order for Content Manager to recognize the skin and dispay its information.\
\
Now what if I want to change a texture on the car ?

First, find the name of the texture you want to change on the car

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (55).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Select the part on the car which has the texture, and find its name, here the livery texture is called skinf150\_massa.png

So I take alonso livery skin, name it skinf150\_massa.png, and put it inside the skin folder of your choice.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (203).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

And when you will switch to the skin of your choice in the Custom Showroom, the livery is changed

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (82).png" alt=""><figcaption><p>No number since Fisichella wasn't a 2011 driver</p></figcaption></figure>

The cool thing about this feature is that you can change any texture of the car on the fly like that. And even the texture of the driver !&#x20;
